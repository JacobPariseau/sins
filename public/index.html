The World Of Party Games is Changing

In the old days you could walk into a dorm party with a deck of cards, sit down, and play a game. But who decides the game? And who decides the rules? You can play King's Cup in Room 231 this week, but next week you're going to walk into Room 316 where they play Kings with a completely different set of rules.

But You Have a Better Game...

…And it DOESN'T depend on everyone agreeing which thirteen rules we're using this week…

…And it DOESN'T require cards, dice, coins, ping pong balls, or twenty extra plastic cups either.

"But those games are party classics!"

It's true. King's Cup, Sociables, Ring of Fire, Circle of Death, President, Ride the Bus, Never Have I Ever -- these are a small sample of hordes of drinking games that have become house party staples.

But it's 2017, and it's time to start acting like it. Take everything you love about those other party games -- the spontaneity, the house rules, the endless nights of fun -- and put them on your phone; that's where Firecup comes in. Imagine never needing another deck of cards.

Create a game and let all your friends join from their own devices. Phone dies? A single device can support multiple players, or even the whole party as needed. Just pass it around.

Are there any other ways to play?

If you're looking for some lighter action, try our Traps variant! There is no event deck and no turns. Each player gets a secret hand of 6 trap cards that they can activate at any time. Instead of discarding them the moment you use them, you keep using them until someone figures out what your card says.

The main Traps variant sends red burn cards at the other players when you trigger a trap, while the Traps Lite variant just notifies them they've been hit with a trap, for more casual nights if you just want to take a drink every time.

Elrond: The ring must be taken deep into Mordor and cast back into the fiery chasm from whence it came. One of you must do this.

Samwise: Take a drink, Mr Elrond sir.

Boromir: One does not simply walk into Mordor. Its black gates are guarded by more than just Orcs. There is evil there that does not sleep. The great Eye is ever watchful. It is a barren wasteland, riddled with fire, ash, and dust. The very air you breathe is a poisonous fume. Not with ten-thousand men could you do this. It is folly.

Legolas: Have you heard nothing Lord Elrond has said? The ring must be destroyed!

Samwise: Oh now Legolas must drink too!

Elrond: Do you intend to say that every time someone utters the word ‘Ring’?

Samwise: Well no Mr Elrond, now that you’ve mentioned it my trap card is no good, see? I’m meant to discard it and draw a new one.

For extra fun, break out Firecup Traps Lite during a movie night, and wait for the movie characters to trigger your traps. When one does, everyone drinks and you swap your trap for a new one. With four people playing, that's 24 chances to catch John McClane take his socks off or hear Vizzini shout in anger. And with hundreds of cards in the deck, it's a new fresh experience every time.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many people can play at once?

Theoretically, unlimited, but play testing has show groups of 4 to 10 people to be optimal.

Do I need to drink alcohol to enjoy this game?

Absolutely not. All it takes is good company. For cards that request drinking, however, we define a 'drink' as homeopathic snake oil diluted to 5% and a 'shot' as the same diluted to 40%.
